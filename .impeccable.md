## Design Context

### Users
People drawn to introspection and self-understanding — not necessarily academic, but thoughtful. They encounter this as a link or a recommendation. Context: quiet, often alone, perhaps late at night. They want to understand something real about themselves, not receive flattery. They may be multilingual (Thai, English, Japanese). They take the experience seriously because the framework invites that.

### Brand Personality
Introspective · dreamlike · precise. Like a journal you've had for years — worn, honest, and yours alone. Not clinical, not cheerful. Quietly significant.

### Aesthetic Direction
OMORI-inspired: cream paper and near-black ink, with OMORI red (#ad1f19) used sparingly as the only accent — specifically for core belief "frames" in the tree. Background carries faint ruled lines (like a journal page) and subtle paper grain texture. Fonts: Bodoni Moda for display/headings, Libre Caslon Text for body, Fira Code for labels/metadata, Caveat for handwriting-feel moments (transition screen). Dark mode ONLY for the transition screen (entering the tree's depths).

Anti-references: anything that feels like a corporate wellness app, Instagram quiz aesthetics, AI-slop gradients, or gamified personality tests.

### Design Principles
1. **Weight through restraint** — every element earns its place. Silence and empty space are part of the design.
2. **Organic, not geometric** — lines wobble slightly, the tree grows with imperfection. Nothing should feel programmatically perfect.
3. **Psychologically honest** — the tree is not decorative. Each visual element maps directly to a psychological dimension. Different trees look meaningfully different.
4. **Journal, not website** — ruled background, serif body text, paper grain. It should feel like writing rather than browsing.
5. **Red means something** — the OMORI red appears only on core belief frames. The user should notice it and wonder.
